Backend release surface requirements
- The Go backend is the system of record for business logic, data integrity, and OpenAPI coverage.
- OpenAPI must remain accurate for all Flutter-called endpoints.
- Any endpoint added or changed must update handler/service behavior, tests, OpenAPI, Flutter integration where shipped, and parity artifacts.
- Stable response contracts and permission checks must remain enforced for launched modules.
- Production deployments must use non-default secrets, real password reset URLs, and validated upload controls.
- Offline/idempotent flows claimed in launch scope must remain supported for POS, purchases/GRN, collections, and expenses where documented.

Primary backend domains supporting SMB launch
- auth, companies, locations, users, roles, permissions
- dashboard, settings, numbering, invoice templates, user preferences
- inventory, products, categories, brands, attributes, combo products
- sales, POS, returns, collections, customers, loyalty, promotions, warranty
- purchases, goods receipts, purchase returns, suppliers, payments
- accounting, cash registers, vouchers, ledgers, reports, audit logs
- HR, attendance, payroll
- workflow, notifications, supportability endpoints

Out-of-scope launch claims
- enterprise treasury and bank-reconciliation depth
- intercompany and consolidation finance
- enterprise IAM and governance posture
- fully commercialized parity for every backend route exposed in OpenAPI
